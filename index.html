What is calisthenics?

Calisthenics is a form of strength training that uses bodyweight exercises to develop control, endurance, and mobility. Instead of relying on machines or heavy weights, you use body weighted movements like push ups, pull ups, dips, and squats to build strength.

The focus is not just about lifting heavier, it's about mastering your body. As you progress, you move into more advanced skills like handstands, muscle-ups, and levers that require coordination, balance, and total body tension.

Why even bother training calisthenics?

It builds real-world strength, improves your mobility, and best of all requires minimal equipment to get started. You don't even need a gym membership, just your body and maybe a pull-up bar to get started.

  • Functional strength: You train movements, not machines.
  • Joint health: Full range-of-motion exercises improve flexibility and control.
  • Scalable difficulty: Every movement has easier and harder variations.

How to get started

Starting is simple. Focus on mastering the fundamental movement patterns first. These are the foundation movements for all future calisthenic progressions.

  • Push: Push ups and dips
  • Pull: Pull ups and bodyweight rows
  • Legs: Squats and lunges
  • Core: Ab routines

If you're unsure how to structure these movements properly, check out the Calisthenics Fundamentals Page where each pattern is explained in detail.

Begin with 3 to 4 workouts per week. Keep your sessions simple:

  1. Warm up (5-10 minutes of light movement and mobility)
  2. Pick 4-5 exercises
  3. Do 3 sets of each
  4. Focus on clean form over high reps

As you get stronger, increase reps, slow down the movement, or progress to harder variations.
The key here is consistency. Mastering the basics first before trying harder progressions and variatons.
