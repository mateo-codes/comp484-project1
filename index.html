What is calisthenics?

Calisthenics is a form of strength training that uses bodyweight exercises to develop control, endurance, and mobility. Instead of relying on machines or heavy weights, you use body weighted movements like push ups, pull ups, dips, and squats to build strength and control over your body.

The focus is not just about lifting heavier, it's about mastering your body. As you progress, you move into more advanced skills like handstands, muscle ups, and front levers that require coordination, balance, and total body tension.

Why even bother training calisthenics?

It builds real world strength, improves your mobility, and best of all requires minimal equipment to get started. You don't even need a gym membership, just your body and maybe a pull up bar to get started.

  • Functional strength: You train movements, not machines.
  • Joint health: Full range of motion exercises improve flexibility and control.
  • Scalable difficulty: Every movement has easier and harder variations.
  • Train anywhere: Home, park, gym, it doesn't matter.

How to get started

Starting is simple. Focus on mastering the fundamental movement patterns first. These are the foundation movements for all future calisthenic progressions.

  • Push: Push ups and dips
  • Pull: Pull ups and bodyweight rows
  • Legs: Squats and lunges
  • Core: L-sit, hanging leg raises, ab routines

If you're unsure how to structure these movements properly, check out the Calisthenics Fundamentals Page where each pattern is explained in detail.

Begin with 3 to 4 workouts per week. Keep your sessions simple:

  1. Warm up: 10 minutes of dynamic movements and mobility stretches
  2. Pick 4 - 5 exercises focusing on a specific muscle group
  3. Do 3 - 4 sets of each exercise
  4. Focus on keeping a clean form and going till failure for each set

As you get stronger, increase reps, slow down the movement, add weight, or progress to harder variations.
The key here is consistency. Mastering the basics will help you build the foundational strength you need to progress and move onto harder skills.
