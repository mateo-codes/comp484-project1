What are the fundamentals of Calisthenics?

Before moving onto learn more complex skills like muscle ups or handstands, it is essential to develop a strong foundation in the core movements. These fundamental exercises train the entire body and prepare your muscles, joints, and nervous system for more advanced progressions.

The 4 main movement patterns

Push
Push ups, dips, and handstand pushups will build your chest, shoulders, and triceps.
Pull
Pull ups, and body rows strengthen both your back and biceps.
Legs
Squats can develop leg strength and mobility.
Core
L-sit, static hollow body holds, and leg raises build up your core strength.

Why Fundamentals Matter

Mastering the basics ensures proper form, reduces injury risk, and builds the strength required for advanced skills. Many beginners rush into advanced movements, but long term progress comes from consistent practice of the fundamentals.

Focusing on clean reps, controlled tempo, and a full range of motion. Strength built slowly and correctly will last a lifetime.

Beginner Starter Routine

  1. Chest: Standard push ups - 3 sets of 8-12 reps
  2. Shoulders: Pike pushups - 3 sets of 8-12 reps
  3. Triceps: Dips - 3 sets of 10-15 reps
  4. Legs: Bodyweight Squats - 3 sets of 12-15 reps
  5. Back: Pull-ups or Assisted Pull-ups - 3 sets of 5-8 reps
  6. Core: Hollow Body Hold - 2 sets of 30-60 seconds
              Hanging leg raises - 3 sets till failure
              L-sit hold - 2 sets till failure

What's Next?

Once you can complete all sets with clean form and full control, it's time to begin progressing your movements. In calisthenics, progress comes from increasing difficulty and not just adding reps.

This may mean trying other forms or push ups, adding weight to your dips, even going from bodyweight squats to trying harder pistol squat progressions.

Really focus on consistency, having proper technique, gradual overload, and having control throughout the whole movement range of an exercise. Mastery of the fundamentals unlocks advanced skills like handstands, muscle ups, front levers, and planches.
Most importantly it's crucial to start trying harder skills regardless of how silly you my think you look.
